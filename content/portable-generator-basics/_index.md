## Portable Generator Basics

Portable generators are commonly used for emergency backup power, outdoor activities, and temporary job site electricity. For users new to generators, understanding the basics is essential before focusing on safety rules or buying decisions.

This guide explains **what portable generators are**, **how they work**, and **what they can realistically power**, using clear, practical explanations.

---

## What Is a Portable Generator?

A portable generator is a self-contained power source that produces electricity using a small internal engine. Unlike permanently installed standby generators, portable generators are **movable, flexible, and designed for temporary use**.

They are typically powered by gasoline, propane, or dual fuel systems and are widely used during short-term power outages or in locations without access to grid electricity.

## Portable Generator vs Other Generator Types

### Portable Generator vs Standby Generator

Standby generators are permanently installed and automatically supply power during outages. Portable generators offer flexibility and lower upfront cost but require manual setup.

## When a Portable Generator Makes Sense

Portable generators are well-suited for:

- Short-term power outages  
- Essential appliance backup  
- Outdoor and recreational use  
- Temporary or mobile power needs  

They are not ideal for whole-home, long-term, or fully automatic power systems.

Understanding these limitations helps prevent unrealistic expectations.
