## Portable Generator Safety: What You Need to Know

Portable generators are widely used during power outages, emergencies, and outdoor activities. While they provide essential backup power, improper use can lead to **serious injuries, carbon monoxide poisoning, fires, and electrical damage**.

This safety hub brings together all essential generator safety guidance in one place. Whether you are a first-time generator owner or have used one for years, understanding these risks is critical to protecting your home, family, and equipment.

---

## Why Generator Safety Matters

Portable generators combine three major hazards:

- **Fuel combustion**
- **Electricity**
- **Exhaust gases**

Most generator-related accidents happen not because of equipment failure, but because of **incorrect placement, poor ventilation, overload, or unsafe operating conditions**.

The guides below address the most common and most dangerous scenarios users encounter.

## Common Generator Safety Mistakes

Many serious incidents result from avoidable mistakes, including:

- Running generators indoors or in garages  
- Ignoring carbon monoxide alarms  
- Operating generators in wet conditions without protection  
- Overloading circuits  
- Refueling while the engine is hot  

Understanding and avoiding these mistakes significantly reduces risk.
